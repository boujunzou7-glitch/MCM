\begin{abstract}
    Wordle is a daily puzzle game from the New York Times.  This paper focuses on data prediction, machine learning and classification problems.  We can confirm the accuracy of our model by ADF, ACF/ PACF and other methods.  We predict the future results of the puzzle using methods such as linear programming.  This paper also illustrates influence difficulty level and attributes of the words have on the result and difficulty grades.
    
    For question 1, the time series prediction ARIMA model and the CRITIC weight model are used.  First we preprocess the data, such as deleting outliers and irrelevant variables.  Then we estimate whether the processed data meets the prerequisite requirements of ARIMA.  Then we get the values of p,d,q by denoising.  Afterwards we can get the reason of the daily change in the number of reported results and the prediction interval of March 1, 2023.  We consider that the attributes of words affect the difficulty of the answer words and thus affect the percentage of scores reported.  We use MOP to determine the four attributes of words.  According to the model, we can find the weight proportion in order to get two equations of difficulty level based on attributes and difficulty level based on scores reported.  After inputting the given data, we are able to ensure that attributes influence the percentage of scores in Hard Mode and degrees of these influence.

    To solve question 2, we introduce the Decision Tree.  Firstly, parameter values are set to establish the Decision Tree.  Then we put two kinds of data obtained in the first question into the model evaluation table to determining the test set and the training set.  We then can evaluate the accuracy of the model.  According to question1, we can get the estimated regression equations of two kinds of difficulty quantification.  Afterwards, we calculate the difficulty level based on estimated regression equation with the given data to predict the score reported for a specific word.  Then we put the word EERIE into our model and get the result.

    For question 3 we use Cluster analysis model (K-means) to classify the difficulty levels.  We import the data into the clustering model to obtain the cluster analysis graph in order to get the model evaluation table.  We get the clustering category order through field difference analysis  and high accuracy of the model confirmed by the values of DBI and CH.

    To solve question 4, we determine to use a Q-Q plot.  We insert the number of guesses per day and find that the data for each number of guesses is normally distributed.

    About our model we discuss the accuracy, advantages and disadvantages.  We consider our model has high accuracy.

\section{Introduction}

\subsection{Background}

Wordle is a daily puzzle offered by the New York Times. Players solve the puzzle by making no more than six guesses at a five-letter actual English word and they gain immediate feedback after making every guess. Players are not allowed to guess words that are unrecognized by the contest. The puzzle includes a regular mode and a “Hard Mode”. Wordle is increasingly popular and has been available in more than sixty languages. We focus exclusively on the English version.

\subsection{Introduction of Wordle}

According to the directions of Wordle posted to the New York Times website, the color of each tile will change once a player has submitted the word \cite{1}. The color represents the accuracy of the player’s guess at each letter in the tiles. If a letter is in a gray tile, it is completely not in the answer. If a letter is in a yellow tile, it is in the wrong location of the answer. If a letter is in a green tile, it is in the answer and the exact location. Hard Mode is more difficult to the players because they have to use the correct letters they have found in previous guesses (letters in green or yellow tiles) to do the following guesses. Wordle offers only one puzzle everyday. If the puzzle can not be solved within six guesses, the players have to wait till the next day for a brand new five-letter English word.

\section{Data preprocessing}
In order to improve the quality, the accuracy and the effectiveness of data, we adopt data cleaning technique. 
\begin{itemize}
    \item {\bf Outliers:} remove the data with an answer word less than 5 letters.
    \item {\bf Sort:} Sort the entire table in ascending order according to "Contest Number".
    \item {\bf Too few reported results:} remove data with less than 10000 reported results.
    \item {\bf Abnormal sum of percentage:} remove data with sum of percentage for score that is greater than 100.
\end{itemize}
\section{Assumptions and Occasions}

We aim to simplify our models and ensure the accuracy of our analyses. Therefore, we make the following assumptions with adequate reasons: 

\begin{itemize}
\item {\bf We ignore answer words in the data that are not five-letter}. Wordle is a puzzle which requires players to guess a five-letter word so we do not take other words without five letter into consideration during our analyses of data.

\item {\bf Data after preprocessing is enough to support our models}. The amount of available data decreased from 359 to 281 after the data pretreatment. The processed data sufficient for us to analyze and establish our models.

\item {\bf All the players have the same subjectivity.}. Individual differences of players are irrelevant and irregular, such as the familiarity to words and standard of English spelling. We exclude these differences between players by default so that we can narrow our focus on the key factors to find the solutions to the problems.

\item {\bf The distributions of scores reported (numbers of guesses) in different modes are about the same}. Every players takes a different amount of guesses in the puzzle. Using these amounts, the percentage of scores reported that shows the distribution can be calculated. According to the percentage we can get the difficulty level of the specific answer word. The calculation will be illustrated in our later analyses. basically the same score reported distributions in two modes can ensure the difficulty level of the answer word always to be consistent, which is conducive to avoiding chaos in data. 
\end{itemize}

\section{Model Establishment and Problem Solutions}

\subsection{Question 1}

In our model, we aim to reach three goals: finding out the reason why the number of reported results changes every day, being able to create a prediction interval for the number of reported results on a specific date (take March 1, 2023 as an instance) and exploring if any attribute of the word influences the percentage of scores reported in Hard Mode. 

 We start with applying the Autoregressive Integrated Moving Average model (ARIMA) to reach our first two goals.

 We then introduce Analytic Hierarchy Process (AHP) and multi-objective programming (MOP). After our calculations and analyses by using Entropy Method and CRITIC weight method, we solve the rest of the problem.

\begin{itemize}
    \item {\bf Test the stability}. 
    
    ARIMA model requires the sequence to satisfy the stability, which means neither the mean value nor the variance of the sequence varies over time. We introduce Augmented Dickey-Fuller test (ADF) to check whether the number of reported results in regular mode fulfill the criteria for a stationary sequence \cite{3}.
    
    Equation based on random walk:
    \begin{equation} \label{eq1}
        X_t = X_t-1 + \omega_t, X_t(0,t{\sigma}^{2})
        % \frac{\partial u}{\partial x} + \frac{\partial v}{\partial y} +\frac{\partial w}{\partial z} =0
        \end{equation}
    One order difference equation of random walk: 
    \begin{equation} \label{eq2}
        \Delta X_t = X_t - X_t-1 = \omega_t ,U_tN(0,{\sigma}^{2} )
    \end{equation} 
    $X_t$ is the position of the random walk at time t.

    The fundamental idea of ADF is based on random walk. We calculate  through regressive computation. If p equals one, the sequence satisfies random walk and it is not stationary.
    
    According to the analysis of t-value, we are able to find out whether t-value can significantly reject the assumption that the sequence is nonstationary, which means that p-value is less than 0.5 .

        \item {\bf Determine the value of p and q}.
        
        In order to do analyses, we create ACF/PACF graphs \cite{4}.

        To begin with, we introduce the autocorrelation equation and partial autocorrelation equation.

        Autocorrelation coefficient equation:
        \begin{equation} \label{eq3}
            ACF(k) = \sum_{t=k+1}^{n}\frac{(Z_t - \overline{Z})(Z_{t-k} - \overline{Z})}{\sum_{t=1}^{n}{(Z_t - \overline{Z})}^{2} } 
        \end{equation}

        Partial autocorrelation coefficient equation:
        \begin{equation} \label{eq4}    
            PACF(k)=\frac{E(Z_t - EZ_t)(Z_{t-k} - EZ_{t-k})}{\sqrt{E{(Z_t - EZ_t)}^{2}}\sqrt{E{(Z_{t-k} - EZ_{t-k})}^{2}}  } = \frac{cov[(Z_t - \overline{Z_t} ),(Z_{t-k} - \overline{Z_{t-k}} )]}{\sqrt{var(Z_t - \overline{Z_t})}\sqrt{var(Z_{t-k} - \overline{Z_{t-k}})}  }        
        \end{equation}

    \item {\bf Develop our model}.
    
    We introduce the CRITIC weight method to give values of weight to attributes \cite{5}.

    For the problem with multiple indicators and objects, the CRITIC weight method can eliminate the influence of some indicators with strong correlations and reduce the overlap of information between indicators. This method is beneficial to get a reliable result. 

    Because we consider that there are multiple attributes influencing the result, we use the CRITIC weight method in order to ensure the accuracy and reliability of our analyses.
    
    Positive index equation:
    
    \begin{equation} \label{eq6}    
        {{x}_{ij}}^{'} = \frac{X_ij-min(X_{1j},X_{nj},···,X_{nj})}{max(X_{1j},X_{nj},···,X_{nj})-min((X_{1j},X_{nj},···,X_{nj}))} 
    \end{equation}

    Negative index equation:   

    \begin{equation} \label{eq7}
        {{x}_{ij}}^{'} = \frac{max(X_{1j},X_{nj},···,X_{nj})-X_ij}{max(X_{1j},X_{nj},···,X_{nj})-min((X_{1j},X_{nj},···,X_{nj}))} 
    \end{equation}

    Index variability equation:

    $$
    \left\{ 
    \begin{matrix}
        \overline{x} & = & \frac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^{n}x_{ij} \\
        S_j & = & \sqrt{\frac{\sum_{n = 1}^{n}(x_ij-\overline{x_j} )^{2} i=1 }{n-1} }2 \\
    \end{matrix}
    \right.
    $$
   
    Index conflict equation:

    \begin{equation} \label{eq9}
    R_j = \sum_{i = 1}^{p}(1-r_ij)      
    \end{equation}

    Information content equation:

    \begin{equation} \label{eq10}
       C_j = S_j\sum_{i = 1}^{p}=S_j\times R_j       
        \end{equation}

    Weight equation:

    \begin{equation} \label{eq7}
        W_j = \frac{C_j}{\sum_{j = 1}^{p}C_j}     
        \end{equation}
